# Enterprise SEO Copywriting Guidelines

## Company Context
**Company Type**: IT Services (AI Engineering & Security)
**Target Geography**: Primarily USA
**Voice**: Top 1% marketer, resourceful, growth-hacking mindset

## Target Audience

### Primary Personas
- **Directors/VPs of IT**
- **Engineering Heads/Managers**

### Key Characteristics
- **Pain Points**: Similar across both personas (read industry forums for insights)
- **Decision Process**:
  - **Enterprises**: Procurement team involvement, committee decisions
  - **Growing Orgs**: Solo decisions, faster cycles
- **Technical Proficiency**: High - they understand engineering trade-offs

## Content Strategy

### Research-Driven Approach
1. **Forum Mining**: Active research on Reddit, Hacker News, Dev.to, LinkedIn
2. **Question-Focused**: Write content that answers questions people ask on:
   - Google searches
   - LLM queries (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
   - Technical forums
3. **Natural Language Queries**: Structure content around conversational search patterns

### Blog Content
- **Written By**: Engineers (technical depth is authentic)
- **Tone**: Technical but accessible, battle-tested insights
- **Format**: Problem → Solution → Implementation
- **SEO Focus**: Answer-driven, long-tail keywords from real user questions

## Growth Hacking Principles

### Long-Term Benefit Focus
1. **Building Authority**: Establish thought leadership in AI engineering
2. **Lead Generation**: Content that naturally drives qualified leads
3. **Low Bounce Rate**: Engaging, valuable content that keeps readers on page
4. **Higher SERPs**: SEO-optimized for sustainable organic growth

### Content Types for Growth
- Deep technical guides (indexed for years)
- Case studies with measurable outcomes
- Problem-solution frameworks
- Tools/frameworks/methodologies that become reference material

## Writing Standards

### Language
- **US English**: Color (not colour), optimize (not optimise), etc.
- **No Regional Nuances**: Generic US market, not Silicon Valley vs. East Coast specific

### Tone & Voice
- **Direct & Honest**: No fluff, get to the point
- **Technical Credibility**: Use precise terminology
- **Action-Oriented**: Focus on outcomes, not features
- **Anti-Consultant**: "We build, not just advise"

### SEO Best Practices
1. **Answer User Intent**: Map content to search/LLM queries
2. **Semantic Keywords**: Natural language, conversational phrases
3. **Technical Depth**: Engineers trust detailed, accurate content
4. **E-E-A-T**: Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness
5. **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)**: Optimize for AI-generated answers

## Messaging Pillars

### Core Value Props
- **Production-First**: Not proof-of-concepts
- **Security Built-In**: AI security from day one
- **Embedded Teams**: Forward-deployed, not remote consultants
- **Battle-Tested**: Real production experience

### Avoid
- ❌ Generic consulting speak
- ❌ Vague promises ("we're the best")
- ❌ Feature lists without context
- ❌ Sales-y language that engineers reject

## Content Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:
- [ ] Answers a real question people ask online
- [ ] Provides actionable value (not just awareness)
- [ ] Uses US English spelling
- [ ] Includes technical credibility markers
- [ ] Optimized for both search engines and LLMs
- [ ] Natural language that engineers would actually use
- [ ] Clear CTA aligned with buyer journey stage
- [ ] Builds long-term authority (not clickbait)

## Metrics That Matter

### Primary Goals
1. **Authority Building**: Backlinks, mentions, thought leadership
2. **Lead Quality**: Directors/VPs/Eng Heads as leads
3. **Engagement**: Low bounce rate, high time on page
4. **SERP Performance**: Top 3 positions for target keywords
5. **Conversion**: Demo requests, strategy calls from qualified prospects

### Secondary Signals
- Social shares in engineering communities
- Reddit/HN discussion references
- LLM citation in responses
- Organic backlinks from technical blogs
